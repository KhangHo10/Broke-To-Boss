Intermediate - Fine-tuning your finances

Building an emergency fund 💱 is crucial for financial security, with the general recommendation being to save 3-6 months of living expenses. Setting savings goals helps prioritize short-term and long-term objectives, such as vacations, buying a new car, or retirement. Understanding how interest rates work, including the difference between simple and compound interest, can help maximize your savings growth over time.

Managing a checking account requires being mindful of fees, avoiding overdrafts, and regularly balancing your account. Direct deposit and electronic transfers offer convenience for receiving income and managing funds. Online and mobile banking tools, such as apps, make it easier to check balances and transfer money efficiently. Budgeting often requires adjustments for irregular expenses, income changes, or new goals. Advanced tracking methods, like budgeting apps or spreadsheets, help monitor income and expenses more closely. Sinking funds are useful for setting aside money for predictable future costs, such as car repairs or holiday spending.
