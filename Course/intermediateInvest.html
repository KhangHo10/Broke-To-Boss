Intermediate - Building Wealth

At the intermediate level, investors delve deeper into various asset classes and investment strategies. This includes exploring the differences between active and passive investing, understanding market cycles 📑, and analyzing investment performance metrics. Intermediate investors learn about diversification to mitigate risk, the impact of inflation on investments, and the significance of asset allocation in building a balanced portfolio. They also begin to explore more complex instruments, such as exchange-traded funds (ETFs) and options, while gaining insights into fundamental and technical analysis to inform their investment choices.

Intermediate investors learn about the difference between active and passive investing. Active investing involves selecting individual securities with the intent of outperforming market indices, while passive investing focuses on replicating market performance through index funds or ETFs. Understanding market cycles, including bull and bear markets, becomes essential as investors recognize how economic conditions can affect their portfolios.

A critical concept at this level is diversification, which helps mitigate risk by spreading investments across different asset classes. Investors learn how to create a balanced portfolio by incorporating various investments that respond differently to market conditions. The impact of inflation on investment returns is also explored, helping investors understand how inflation can erode purchasing power over time.
